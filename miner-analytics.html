Sector Context

The miner-to-HPC trade has clearly become the dominant macro narrative. Operators that have signed credible HPC contracts, including TeraWulf with $12.8B from Fluidstack and Core42, Core Scientific with $10B+ anchored by CoreWeave, Cipher Mining with three hyperscale leases, and IREN with a 5 GW Nvidia DSX deployment, are being rewarded with multi-bagger price action and aggressive analyst target raises. JPMorgan upgraded Cipher and CleanSpark while trimming Marathon and Riot, sharpening the divide between hybrid AI miners and pure-play bitcoin operators.

Macro tailwinds are mixed. Bitcoin has reclaimed $80,000 even as banks scrap Fed rate-cut forecasts, with the FOMC holding at 3.5 to 3.75% under the most divided vote since 1992. Powell's term ends May 15 and Kevin Warsh, expected to take the gavel, leans hawkish. The CLARITY Act yield compromise and the OCC's GENIUS Act stablecoin rulemaking are nudging digital asset rails further into mainstream finance.

On the other side of the ledger, Soluna's nearly 29 million share resale registrations remain a textbook example of how dilution can offset clear operational progress. Mawson's rebrand to Big Digital Energy under a Nasdaq listing review adds idiosyncratic risk to the small-cap sleeve. Investors continue to differentiate aggressively between contracted, financed HPC pivots and operators still stuck on the bitcoin-only treadmill.
