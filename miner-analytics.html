Public miners are now collectively credited with locking in tens of billions of dollars in long-duration HPC revenue, with TeraWulf alone reporting over $12.8B in contracted backlog after Google-backed Fluidstack and Core42 deals. Industry analysts continue to argue that the AI and HPC line of business is on track to make up as much as 70% of listed-miner revenue by year-end, a re-rating that is showing up in YTD share-price performance well above Bitcoin's own gain.

Macro is the main pressure point. The Fed held rates at 3.5-3.75% on April 29, with the committee unusually divided and inflation still elevated by oil prices that have surged more than 60% since end-February. The Hormuz-driven oil shock is forcing the higher-for-longer signaling that has weighed on speculative assets. Bitcoin closed April near $75,684 after a roughly 13% monthly gain, but ETF outflows accelerated into month end and Treasury yields hit fresh July-2025 highs.

Expect company-specific catalysts to dominate over the coming week. SLNH continues to ramp Project Dorothy. MIGI/Big Digital Energy's leadership reset will be tested at first earnings. WULF's primary task is converting backlog into recognized revenue.
