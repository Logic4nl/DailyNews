Sector Context

The miner-to-HPC group traded with a clear upside bias on April 21 as three converging forces drove dispersion between pure-play HPC hosts and laggard pivots. Contract flow was front of mind: Core Scientific added a 570 MW Phase 3 block with CoreWeave, Applied Digital extended Ellendale by 150 MW, and TeraWulf quietly pulled the Hawesville substation energization date forward by several weeks. Taken together these prints strengthen the view that hyperscaler appetite for contracted, liquid-cooled capacity remains undersupplied at the one to two year horizon and that the miners with permitted land, transmission and experienced teams are the ones monetising first.

The three core positions moved together for different reasons. WULF benefited from the incremental Hawesville acceleration and continued digestion of the $900M April raise. SLNH is riding tight-float momentum behind the West Texas wind program and the now-public Dorothy 1A structure, which rationalises the balance sheet heading into a Project Sophie decision. MIGI is still finding its feet under new leadership, but the first insider buy from Executive Chairman Kilgore is exactly the signal the market was waiting for and provides a proxy for how the reconstituted board views fair value.

Counterforces remain. Bitcoin held in the mid $80k range through the session and mining economics continue to pressure the laggards that have not yet secured HPC revenue. Cleanspark and Bitfarms both slid modestly on a day when peers rallied, a pattern consistent with capital rotation into the contracted-capacity names. Activism tailwinds are building too, with Starboard pressing Riot to commit to measurable Corsicana milestones before the Q3 2026 update. The playbook for the rest of Q2 is clear: contract announcements, insider buys from reconstituted boards, and Q1 print traffic in early May will set the next layer of dispersion.
