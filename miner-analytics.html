How to read today's data

Column legend

The first columns are basics. Ticker carries its tier badge (CORE for your holdings, PIVOT for AI / HPC pivot peers, BTC for pure miners). Last is yesterday's close. Day %, 5D % and 1M % give you the trend windows.

RSI(D) is the daily 14-period Relative Strength Index, where 30 is oversold, 50 is neutral, 70 is overbought, 80+ is extreme. RSI(W) is the same on a weekly bar, slower and more telling about the regime than the daily noise. MACD ↑ means the MACD line is above its signal line (rising momentum); ↓ means below (fading). MA Stack X/4 counts how many of the 20, 50, 100 and 200 day moving averages the price sits above.

Setup (0-100) answers "is this a clean entry today?" Sweet spot is RSI 50-65 with confirming volume and a tightening Bollinger squeeze. Pivot Conviction (0-100) answers "is this acting like a real AI / HPC infrastructure stock or a re-themed BTC miner?" Blends MA stack, volume, outperformance vs the BTC-only basket and breakouts above structure. Hype Stage (Quiet to Distribution) is the retail-cycle classifier. Sentiment is the StockTwits + Reddit composite label, with "Retail euphoria" the most extreme.

How to use the three scores together

Setup tells you about entry timing. Pivot Conviction tells you about thesis quality and position sizing. Hype Stage tells you about exit timing.

Clean add = Setup > 60 AND Hype Stage 0-1 AND Pivot Conviction > 60. Today, no name meets that combination because Hype Stage is 1-4 across the board. Today is not an entry day.

Clean trim = Hype Stage 3+ on a name you're already long. HUT at Distribution is the cleanest. CORZ at Mania is next. WULF, CIFR and IREN at "Hot" sit one rung below; trim ladder reads heaviest at Distribution, less aggressive at Mania, watch trail stops at Hot.

Clean hold = Pivot Conviction high AND Hype not yet at 3. SLNH and BGDE both fit. These are the names you give the most room to run.
