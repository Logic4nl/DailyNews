Sector Context

The Bitcoin mining sector's transformation into AI infrastructure continues to accelerate in mid-April 2026. The defining story this week is capital allocation: TeraWulf's $900M equity raise, Hut 8's $7B lease deal with Anthropic backed by Google, and Soluna's $53M wind farm acquisition each represent different strategies for the same end goal - converting energy assets into AI compute capacity.

A clear valuation gap has emerged between miners with secured HPC contracts (trading at 12.3x EV/NTM sales) and pure-play Bitcoin miners (5.9x), incentivizing every company in the sector to announce AI pivot plans. CoinShares reports that public miners sold over 15,000 BTC in recent months to fund these transitions, marking what some analysts call the "end of the HODL era" for public mining companies.

Power remains the bottleneck. With data center rack densities exceeding 100kW and new facility delays mounting across the US, miners with existing power purchase agreements and grid connections hold a structural advantage. IREN's 4.5GW renewable portfolio and Soluna's vertically integrated wind-to-compute model represent the emerging playbook.

Bitcoin itself trades near $73,900, struggling at the $75K resistance level. The Iran ceasefire optimism provides a macro tailwind, but the $424M in futures liquidations this week highlight continued uncertainty. For miners, the BTC price matters less each quarter as HPC revenue share grows - a fundamental shift that is reshaping how investors value these companies.
