Sector Context

The BTC miner-to-HPC transition accelerated dramatically in early 2026, with over $70 billion in cumulative AI/HPC contracts announced across publicly listed mining companies. TeraWulf's $900 million equity raise underscores the capital intensity of the pivot, as miners build out data center campuses requiring hundreds of millions in upfront investment before generating HPC revenue. Hashprice dropped to approximately $28-30 per PH/s/day, forcing listed miners to confront the reality that pure-play bitcoin mining is increasingly uneconomic at current difficulty levels. Companies with power assets, land and fiber connectivity, such as WULF, HUT and CORZ, are commanding premium valuations as they transform into digital infrastructure platforms. Smaller operators without HPC pipelines face existential pressure.

The bifurcation is starkest in the numbers: Core Scientific already derives 39% of revenue from AI hosting, TeraWulf is at 27%, and both expect to exceed 70% by year-end. The sector collectively sold over 15,000 BTC from peak treasury levels to fund infrastructure buildouts. The April 2024 halving's impact continues to compress margins, with weighted average mining cash costs near $80,000 versus bitcoin spot around $75,000. The next halving around 2028 will cut the block subsidy further to 1.5625 BTC, making the HPC pivot not merely strategic but existential for public miners that intend to survive as standalone entities.
