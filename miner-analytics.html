Sector Context

The bitcoin miner cohort continues to trade as an AI infrastructure play first and a bitcoin proxy second. CoinShares and S&P Global both flag a structural pivot away from pure self-mining, with public miners now collectively sitting on more than $70 billion of announced AI/HPC contracts. Operators with secured HPC contracts trade at roughly 12.3x EV/NTM sales, while pure-play miners sit at 5.9x. CoinDesk reports public miners have offloaded more than 15,000 BTC since the start of the year to fund the pivot, ending the hold-everything era.

Among the three core positions, TeraWulf carries the strongest narrative tailwind on the back of $12.8B of AI hosting contracts, the $900M Kentucky-data-centre raise, and the Morgan Stanley overweight with a $41.50 price target. The 8 May Q1 earnings call is the key near-term catalyst. Soluna trades on a much more fragile setup: positive Nasdaq compliance news on 1 May was overwhelmed by overhang from 29M registered resale shares, and free cash flow remains negative. Mawson's 24 April rebrand to Big Digital Energy reset the equity story but the stock is still 84.7 percent below its 52-week high.

Across the peer set, the IREN / CIFR / CORZ trio remains the model for an HPC-anchored mining business, with IREN scaling to 140,000 GPUs, CIFR running off a $9.3B backlog and CORZ extending its CoreWeave deal to $10.2B over 12 years. Riot's AMD expansion and Hut 8's Google-linked $3B bond deal both confirm the secular shift. Risks: oil-driven inflation has parked the Fed at 3.50-3.75 percent and energy-cost pressure is squeezing the names without locked-in contracts. Marathon and other pure miners remain caught between bitcoin-price beta and a tougher hosting power-cost curve.
