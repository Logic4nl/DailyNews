Sector Context

The bitcoin miner-to-HPC sector is experiencing a powerful rally driven by two converging tailwinds: bitcoin's surge above $76,000 to a two-month high, and accelerating institutional demand for AI data center capacity. The Oracle-Bloom Energy 2.8 GW fuel cell deal underscores the desperation for power solutions, while Goldman Sachs' forecast of 220% data center power demand growth by 2030 validates the long-term thesis.

More than half of planned US data center builds have been delayed or canceled, making miners with existing power infrastructure and grid connections increasingly valuable. Companies like IREN, RIOT, HUT and CORZ that have announced AI power redirects are being rerated by the market. Cantor Fitzgerald's $30 price target for WULF reflects this dynamic.

The broader macro backdrop is mixed. Oil above $120/bbl from the Hormuz blockade creates energy cost headwinds but also highlights the value of locked-in power purchase agreements. BTC's resilience despite geopolitical uncertainty suggests institutional flows are providing a floor. The GENIUS Act stablecoin regulation and coordinated global crypto frameworks from Japan, Hong Kong, South Korea and the US are bringing regulatory clarity that benefits the entire digital asset ecosystem.
