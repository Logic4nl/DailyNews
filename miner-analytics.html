Sector Context

Thursday produced the single largest day of AI infrastructure contract flow in the miner-to-HPC complex this cycle. APLD's 300 MW, 15-year Delta Forge 1 lease with an undisclosed US hyperscaler (approximately $7.5B lifetime contracted revenue) and Hut 8's concurrently announced $7B, 15-year River Bend Louisiana agreement together commit more than $14B of new backlog in a single session. Add Cipher's Fluidstack LOI for 50 MW at Stingray TX, IREN's confirmation that its Sweetwater substation energized on schedule, and Bitfarms's decision to rebrand to Keel Infrastructure with a New York HQ, and the pivot narrative that has dominated the sector for nine months crystallised further into a structural re-rating event for the names with definitive customer commitments.

Core position performance diverged sharply. WULF traded softer after the dust settled from the April 15 $900M raise but the underlying story is strengthening with Hawesville 250 MW tracking late Q2 / early Q3 energization and an upcoming Q1 print on May 6. SLNH pulled back 4.5% on the 424(b)(3) resale prospectus covering 26.5M shares, a visible overhang even if no new capital is raised. MIGI fell 8.3% on heavy volume as investors digested the combined rebrand to Big Digital Energy, the imminent BGDE ticker change on April 30, and the Nasdaq delist determination, even though the new board continues to signal confidence through open-market purchases. The pattern holds: names with contracted hyperscaler revenue are being paid for execution while names still negotiating are being penalised for timing.

Looking ahead, the RIOT board's response to Starboard sets a tangible end-Q3 2026 milestone for a Corsicana HPC conversion roadmap, a useful deadline that could anchor activist negotiations. Bitdeer's March operations update (70 EH/s mining, $43M AI cloud ARR growing 105% month-on-month) establishes it as a credible dual-track operator. Bitcoin held the $86-88k range throughout the session, contributing no tailwind from mining cost-per-coin economics but reducing downside risk. Into the first week of May the lineup of catalysts is dense: Q1 prints from HUT (May 8), RIOT (May 8), WULF (May 6), BITF/KEEL rebrand effectiveness (May 5), BGDE ticker change (April 30). The market is asking for more of what it saw today, and the names that deliver signed contracts are the ones being paid.
