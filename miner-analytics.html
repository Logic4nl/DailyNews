Sector Context

The miner-to-HPC complex extended Monday and Tuesday gains into a broad-based but uneven rally on April 22 as the sector leaned further into the pivot narrative. Ten of 15 tracked tickers closed green with all three core positions participating. Contract and pre-contract flow continued at an elevated pace: IREN pre-leased another 50 MW at Childress, Marathon disclosed its first 25 MW HPC pilot at Garden City, Cipher locked a 60 MW ERCOT power purchase agreement at Black Pearl, and Bitdeer broke ground on a 50 MW Rockdale Phase 2 speculative build. Taken together this was one of the busiest 24-hour stretches for contracted-capacity announcements this year and validates the view that hyperscaler and second-tier cloud demand for near-term liquid-cooled power remains supply-constrained through 2026.

Core position performance is diverging on idiosyncratic catalysts rather than beta. WULF firmed on an incremental Hawesville commissioning update and the placing of its Q1 earnings date on May 6, giving the market a clear near-term checkpoint. SLNH advanced on the preliminary proxy filing which crystallises the June 11 Dorothy 1A buyout vote and sets a Project Sophie advisory vote that could finally bound the HPC pivot question. MIGI extended Monday's Kilgore insider buy with a Stanley buy on Tuesday, an unusually strong two-day signalling pattern under a week-old leadership team, and a constructive proof point for investors trying to calibrate fair value post-board reconstitution.

Laggards got pushed. CLSK fell 3.4% on the HC Wainwright downgrade, a reminder that the market is willing to re-rate names that fail to progress beyond strategy reviews. RIOT drifted lower as Starboard's Q4 2026 ultimatum raised the spectre of a 2027 proxy fight, while APLD pulled back on CEO Cummins's planned 10b5-1 sale despite the underlying Ellendale story remaining intact. Bitcoin's steady tape in the $86k-$88k band took tailwind out of the mining cost-per-coin trade and concentrated attention on contract milestones instead. Into month end the read-through is that the names with definitive agreements, insider buying and board clarity continue to outperform the names still promising them.
