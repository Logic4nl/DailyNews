Sector Context

The BTC miner-to-HPC pipeline is accelerating dramatically in late March 2026. The post-halving economics combined with surging energy costs from the Strait of Hormuz crisis have created a punishing environment for pure-play miners, forcing a reckoning across the sector. MARA reported a $1.71B net loss for Q4 2025, while public miners collectively sold over 15,000 BTC to fund their AI pivots. The new value metric has shifted from hashrate and BTC treasury to "Power Capacity," with companies like CORZ, WULF and IREN emerging as standard-bearers. IREN's $9.7B Microsoft deal and Core Scientific's $8.7B CoreWeave agreement represent landmark contracts. Meanwhile, smaller players like SLNH and MIGI are pursuing differentiated paths—Soluna with wind-powered behind-the-meter data centers and Mawson through strategic M&A exploration. Bitfarms' CEO declaration that the company is "no longer a Bitcoin company" may be the most telling signal of the sector's transformation. BTC at $69-72K provides a stable backdrop, but the real alpha is in HPC infrastructure capacity.
